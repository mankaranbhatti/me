Coronavirus pandemic, a rare catastrophe unlike anything experienced in our lifetimes, has changed the world dramatically resulting in a tragical loss of masses of human lives. With the necessary implementation of quarantines and social distancing practices to contain this pandemic, the whole globe has been put in a confinement i.e. the “LOCKDOWN”. This unusual crisis and lack of proficiency at understanding the epidemiology of the virus has made it difficult to understand and foresee the effectiveness of such safer measures and methods of containment which has adversely affected people’s lives and livelihoods. The emerging global world economy lacks the corresponding social and economic structures that could cushion the vicissitudes of fortune resulting from the magnitude and velocity of such a collapsion. Worldwide, policymakers are confronted with numerous crises and are trying to provide unprecedented support to households, firms, and financial markets. While there is a possibility of strong recovery with such steps of planning yet there is a great uncertainty about what the economic climate will be like when this holocaust has passed. With the assumption that this pandemic summits in the second quarter for most of the world and diminishes in the second half of this year, a prestigious organisation – “International Monetary Fund” in their publish of April’s ‘World Economic Outlook’  have projected a fall to -3% in 2020, making this great Lockdown the worst recession since the Great Depression which just plummeted to -0.1%.
